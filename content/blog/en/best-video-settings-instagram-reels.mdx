Instagram, like every platform, re-compresses what you upload. The trick to sharp Reels isn't a secret filter — it's handing Instagram a file it barely has to touch.

## Aim for these

- **Aspect:** 9:16, full-frame. Avoid baked-in letterbox bars.
- **Resolution:** 1080 × 1920. Higher than 1080p gets downscaled by Instagram's own (not-great) resampler, so downscale it yourself with a good one first.
- **Frame rate:** 30 fps is the safe default; keep 60 fps only if the motion really needs it.
- **Bitrate:** generous but sane — a clean H.264 file around 8–12 Mbps gives Instagram's encoder plenty to work with.
- **Format:** MP4 / H.264 High / AAC audio.

## Why "more pixels" can look worse

If you upload a 4K Reel, Instagram downscales it to ~1080p with a fast internal scaler that can add shimmer and aliasing. Downscaling to exactly 1080p yourself, with a high-quality resampler, gives a cleaner result that survives Instagram's pass better.
