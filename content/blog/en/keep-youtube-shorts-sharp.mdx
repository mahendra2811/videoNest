YouTube is the opposite of WhatsApp. Its encoder is good, and it **rewards a high-quality source** — the more clean signal you give it, the better its own re-encode looks.

## Give YouTube more, not less

- **Resolution:** keep it high. 1080p, 1440p, even 4K sources are fine — YouTube's transcoder uses the extra detail.
- **Bitrate:** go generous. A high-bitrate source gives YouTube's VP9/AV1 ladder more to preserve.
- **Frame rate:** keep your native rate (30 or 60 fps).
- **Aspect:** 9:16 for Shorts, full-frame.

## Don't pre-shrink it

For WhatsApp you downscale to protect the size budget. For YouTube, **don't** — shrinking throws away detail YouTube could have kept. VideoNest's YouTube profiles pass your resolution through (up to 1440p in the browser) and use a higher bitrate on purpose.

## In VideoNest

Pick **YouTube Shorts** (or **YouTube** for landscape). You can choose a quality target — 1080p, 1440p or 4K (capped to your source, never upscaled) — and even AV1/HEVC when your browser supports it. Then upload from the YouTube app.
