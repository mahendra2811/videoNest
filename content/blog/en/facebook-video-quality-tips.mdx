Facebook re-encodes uploads like everyone else, and feed video and Stories get treated a little differently.

## Feed video (landscape)

- **Aspect:** 16:9 works well in the feed.
- **Resolution:** 1080p is the sweet spot; bigger gets downscaled.
- **Bitrate:** a clean, generously-bitrated H.264 file gives Facebook's encoder a good base.

## Stories (vertical)

- **Aspect:** 9:16, full-frame.
- **Resolution:** 1080 × 1920.

## The same principle everywhere

You can't stop Facebook from compressing — but a correctly-shaped, sensibly-bitrated MP4 means its re-encode has little to fix, so your video stays sharp. VideoNest has dedicated **Facebook Video** and **Facebook Story** profiles that do this on your device.
